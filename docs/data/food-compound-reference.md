# Food Compound Reference

This document defines medically relevant food compounds used by the app to classify foods and detect potential symptom triggers.

The data in this file should only be updated using reliable medical or scientific sources. Examples include Monash University FODMAP research and clinical histamine intolerance literature.

These classifications are intentionally conservative and should prioritize accuracy over completeness.

## High FODMAP foods
source: https://www.monashfodmap.com/about-fodmap-and-ibs/high-and-low-fodmap-foods

- Vegetables
    - artichoke
    - asparagus
    - garlic
    - green peas
    - leek
    - mushrooms
    - onion
    - red capsicum (bell pepper)
- Fruits 
    - apples
    - cherries
    - dried fruit
    - mango
    - nectarines
    - peaches
    - pears
    - plums
    - watermelon
- Dairy & alternatives
    - cow's milk
    - custard
    - evaporated milk
    - ice cream
    - soy milk
    - sweetened condensed milk
    - yoghurt
- Protein sources
    - Most legumes
- Breads & cereals
    - Wheat/rye/barley based products
- Sugars, sweeteners & confectionery
    - high fructose corn syrup
    - honey
    - sugar free confectionery
- Nuts & seeds
    - cashews
    - pistachios
