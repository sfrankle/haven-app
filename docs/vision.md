# Haven — Vision & Principles

## What it is

Haven is a personal notebook with intelligence — a private space for noticing patterns in daily life.

You log what you eat, how you feel, what you do, and how you slept. Haven quietly finds the connections. The goal isn't to tell you what to do — it's to surface the data that confirms what you already suspect: the things that are supposed to make you feel better actually do.

**Litmus phrase:** a spellbook you could show your doctor.

---

## Who it's for

For anyone living in a body that sometimes surprises them.

For people who know the basics help — sleep, water, movement, rest — but live in a world that makes them easy to forget. For people who can feel a pattern before they can name it. For people who want proof, not advice.

Haven is designed to be personal without being so personal that a stranger couldn't pick it up.

---

## What it is not

- Not a calorie tracker or behavior enforcement tool
- Not a scoring system or streak app
- Not a social platform
- Not a cloud service — data stays on device
- Not a diagnostic tool — insights are observations, not prescriptions

---

## Core product loop

```
Log fast  →  See patterns  →  Get proof the good stuff works
```

1. **Log fast** — every entry type reachable in ≤3 taps from the main screen
2. **See patterns** — automatic correlation discovery; the app tells you what it notices
3. **Act on it** — reinforcement that the small things — rest, water, movement, a moment outside — actually do what they're supposed to

The logging has no value without the patterns. The patterns have no value without enough logging. Both are core to the product.

---

## Design principles

**No pressure.** No scores, no streaks, no "you missed 3 days." Missing days is fine. The app holds space without friction.

**Calm by default.** Muted palette, minimal motion, quiet confirmations. "Saved." is enough.

**Exploration over prescription.** Insights are observations: "You logged bloating 7/10 times after dairy." Never: "You should cut dairy."

**Privacy-first.** All data on device. No accounts. No network calls. No analytics or crash reporting that sends anything off-device.

**Low friction.** Tap-first over type-first. Surface recent and frequent options. No required setup before first use. Notifications (when they exist) are context-triggered, not repetitive, and use varied language to avoid habituation.
